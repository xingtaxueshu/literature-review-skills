# Example: From Topic To Research Gap

## Starting Topic

Broad topic: rural e-commerce participation.

Recommended anchor question: why do farmers with similar external conditions differ in sustained participation in rural e-commerce?

## Step 1: Refine The Question

Narrow the topic from "whether farmers adopt rural e-commerce" to "what explains continued use after initial adoption." Keep the unit of analysis clear: farmers facing broadly similar infrastructure, market access, and policy exposure.

## Step 2: Decompose Review Modules

- Economic incentives: income expectations, price premiums, transaction costs, and risk.
- Capability threshold: digital literacy, product standardization, packaging, and customer-service skills.
- Social networks: peer learning, village brokers, cooperatives, kinship ties, and trust.
- Institutional/platform environment: subsidies, training, logistics support, platform rules, and local government programs.

## Step 3: Compare Research Methods

| Method | Useful For | Limitation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Survey/regression | Estimating associations between farmer traits, support conditions, and participation outcomes | May miss process mechanisms and informal constraints |
| Interviews | Revealing motivations, learning pathways, and perceived barriers | Hard to generalize without careful sampling |
| Panel data | Tracking adoption, dropout, and sustained participation over time | Requires repeated observations and consistent measures |
| Case comparison | Explaining why similar villages or farmers diverge | Sensitive to case selection and boundary definition |

## Step 4: Map Contradictions

| Issue | Literature Claim A | Literature Claim B | Review Implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Logistics cost | High delivery cost suppresses rural e-commerce participation | Aggregation, cooperatives, or platform support can neutralize cost pressure | Ask when logistics cost becomes decisive rather than assuming a uniform effect |
| Digital literacy | Low digital literacy blocks participation | Intermediaries and family members can compensate for weak individual skills | Distinguish individual capability from household or network capability |
| Subsidies | Subsidies encourage entry and lower adoption barriers | Short-term subsidies can create dependence without sustained participation | Separate initial adoption effects from post-adoption persistence |

## Step 5: Locate The Research Gap

Existing studies often explain initial adoption by emphasizing infrastructure, subsidies, and basic digital skills. A sharper gap is the mechanism of sustained participation after initial adoption: why some farmers continue to sell, learn, adapt, and reinvest while others withdraw even under similar external conditions. This points to interaction effects among incentives, capability accumulation, networks, and platform or institutional support.

## What To Write Next

Write a compact review section that moves from adoption factors to persistence mechanisms, then define sustained participation, identify likely mediating mechanisms, and justify a method that can observe change over time or compare divergent cases.
